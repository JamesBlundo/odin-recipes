Fettucini Alfredo

Description

Fettuccine Chicken Alfredo is a rich and creamy Italian-American classic that combines tender fettuccine pasta with a velvety Alfredo sauce made from butter, cream, and Parmesan cheese. The dish is typically paired with seasoned, pan-seared chicken, which adds a savory protein element to balance the smooth, decadent sauce. This comforting dish is beloved for its simplicity and satisfying flavor, making it a favorite among pasta lovers.

To make Fettuccine Chicken Alfredo, start by cooking fettuccine pasta until al dente. While the pasta cooks, season chicken breasts with salt and pepper, then sear them in a skillet until golden brown and cooked through. In another pan, melt butter and add heavy cream, simmering the mixture until it thickens slightly. Stir in Parmesan cheese until smooth, then combine the cooked pasta and sliced chicken with the sauce. Toss everything together and garnish with parsley or extra Parmesan if desired. Serve hot and enjoy!

Ingredients

  • 2 boneless, skinless chicken breasts
  • 12 oz fettuccine pasta
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • 1 cup heavy cream
  • 1 cup grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Cook fettuccine according to package instructions. Drain and set aside.
  2. Season chicken with salt and pepper. In a skillet over medium heat, cook chicken for about 6-7 minutes per side, or until fully cooked. Slice into strips.
  3. In another pan, melt butter over medium heat. Add heavy cream and bring to a simmer. Let it cook for 2-3 minutes until slightly thickened.
  4. Stir in Parmesan cheese and cook until the sauce is smooth. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Add the cooked pasta and chicken into the sauce, tossing to coat evenly.
